Dataset

We pair the NED framework with the dataset, NED-ImageNet (a curated subset of ImageNet-22k). NED-ImageNet consists of a pretraining dataset and 5 sequences of images. For pretraining we use the standard ImageNet-1K. This allows us to leverage existing models built by the community as pre-trained checkpoints. Sequences’ images come from ImageNet-22K after removing ImageNet-1K’s images. Each sequence contains images from 1000 classes, 750 of which do not appear in ImageNet-1K. We refer to the overlapping 250 classes as Pretrain classes and the remaining 750 as Novel classes. Each sequence is constructed by randomly sampling images from a heavy-tailed distribution of these 1000 classes and contains around 90,0000 samples. We consider classes with less than 50 samples to be in the tail of the distribution and classes with 50 or more samples to be in the head. This sequence composition is constructed to study the effects of methods on combinations of pretrain vs novel, and head vs tail classes.
